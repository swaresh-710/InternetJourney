Early Age of the Internet

In the early days of the internet, a groundbreaking era known as Web1 emerged with birth of the World Wide Web. Sir Tim Berners-Lee in the late 1980s introduced key technologies like Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP) and Hypertext Markup Language (HTML), changed the way information was accessed and shared. With the introduction of web browsers like Mosaic and Netscape Navigator, the web became accessible to the general public. Web1 brought an abundance of websites that became the gateways to a world of knowledge. Organizations, businesses, and individuals could create their own web pages and share information globally. The concept of online directories, such as Yahoo!, emerged, providing users with categorized lists of websites and making it easier to navigate the expanding digital landscape. Email became a ubiquitous form of communication during the Web1 era. People could exchange messages instantly, regardless of their physical location. The concept of online forums and discussion boards further facilitated communication and knowledge sharing. It also Introduced Search engines, which became the go-to tools for finding information on the vast web and thanks to Web1 we got concept of E-commerce and Online Shopping. Though it created foundation of whatever we are able to do with it today, it still was just 1st iteration and like our pose it is never perfect in 1st, 2nd, 3rd attempt.

The early age of the internet, also known as Web1, refers to the initial phase of the World Wide Web and its development.

During this period, the internet was primarily used for basic communication and information sharing. Key features of this era include:

  • Dial-up internet connections
  • HTML and early web browsers like Mosaic and Netscape Navigator
  • Static web pages with limited interactivity
  • Websites with simple designs and minimal graphics

Web1 laid the foundation for the rapid expansion and evolution of the internet, setting the stage for future advancements in technology, communication, and online experiences.

Web2: Rise of User Generated Content

  • Web2 refers to the second generation of the World Wide Web, characterized by the shift from static websites to dynamic and interactive user-generated content.
  • Key features of Web2 include social media platforms, online collaboration tools, and user participation through comments, likes, and sharing.
  • Web2 brought about a paradigm shift in internet usage, empowering individuals to create, share, and collaborate on a global scale.
  • Examples of popular Web2 platforms include Facebook, Twitter, YouTube, and Wikipedia.

Web3 because you own it

Web3 was developed as a response to the limitations of Web2, aiming to address various issues and provide a more decentralized and user-centric internet experience.
Web2 platforms are typically centralized, control are concentrated in the hands of a few entities. In Web2, users often face a lack of control over their personal information and limited transparency regarding how their data is used.

It seeks to solve problems related to centralized control, lack of privacy, and data ownership, enabling individuals to have more control over their online presence and transactions.
Web3 aims to decentralize control by leveraging blockchain technology and decentralized networks. It empowers users by enabling peer-to-peer interactions, eliminating intermediaries, and allowing individuals to have more ownership and control over their data and digital assets.

Web3 works by leveraging blockchain technology, decentralized networks, and smart contracts to enable peer-to-peer interactions, secure transactions, and the creation and ownership of digital assets.
